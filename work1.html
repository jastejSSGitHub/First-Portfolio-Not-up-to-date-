Overview

This game was my first attempt at learning to build 2D platformer games, which I had been obsessed with since my childhood addiction with Nintendo old school games. Being an avid video game player, I based this game on the greek mythology inspired by the extremely famous "God of War" game on the playstation platform,which was my all time favourite game. This 2D platformer was about a greek hero named "Kronos" who defends his city against the creatures summoned from the hell beneath his city.
Learning game design became extremely useful after I learnt how to implement a combination of puzzle solving, collecting coins and health jumping on complex shaped and sized bocks and defeating villain bosses. Read further for more details on game design and creation.The whole movement of the player with keys was functioned by trigonometric functions throughout the code which made the running style and dynamics realistic with proper movement of arms and legs while running instead of a static player image.

Process Analysis

This entire environment was build in the Processing IDE, a super powerful tool for Java programming and getting animation based results using mathematical functions and equations including trigonometry. I started by building a layout of the whole game and different level designs by planning the cordinates of all the blocks you see in the gameplay.

There are two levels in the game, with increased difficulties and we as the hero play the game by beating and escaping the creatures from hell in our way. The second planning that went in the project was the unique placement of coins throughout the level design on the blocks with intelligently placed cordinatesto challenge the user.
